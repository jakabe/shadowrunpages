<p>
>Shadow Slang
</p>

<p>>breeder n. Ork slang for a “normal” human.</p>
<p>>buzz v. Go away. Buzz off.</p>
<p>>chill adj. Good, cool, acceptable.</p>
<p>>chip truth n. A fact or honest statement.</p>
<p>>chipped adj. Senses, skills, reflexes, muscles, and so on, enhanced
by cyberware.</p>
<p>>chrome n. Cyberware, especially obvious enhancements.</p>
<p>>chummer n. Friend, used in the same sense as “pal” or “buddy.”</p>
<p>>clip n. A box magazine for a firearm.</p>
<p>>comm n. Short for commlink, your phone, handheld computer, music
player, game device, and more in the palm of your hand.</p>
<p>>corp n. Corporation. adj. Corporate.</p>
<p>>cred n. Money. Reputation, especially good reputation.</p>
<p>>dandelion eater n. (vulgar) An elf.</p>
<p>>dataslave n. Corporate decker or other data-processing employee.</p>
<p>>datasteal n. Theft of data from a computer, usually by decking.</p>
<p>>deck n. A cyberdeck. v. To use a cyberdeck, usually illegally.</p>
<p>>decker n. A person who illegally uses a cyberdeck.</p>
<p>>deckhead n. Simsense abuser.</p>
<p>>drek n. (vulgar) Feces. A common curse word.</p>
<p>>dump v. To be involuntarily ejected from the Matrix.</p>
<p>>dumpshock n. The painful sensation of being forcibly ejected from the
Matrix while deeply involved in multi-sensory interactions.</p>
<p>>exec n. A corporate executive.</p>
<p>>frag v. (vulgar) Common swear word referring to the act of copulation.</p>
<p>>fragged adj. (vulgar) Broken, in trouble.</p>
<p>>geek v. To kill.</p>
<p>>go-gang n. A vehicular gang.</p>
<p>>hacker n. Someone who illegally interacts with the Matrix, either by
using a cyberdeck (as a “decker”) or with the power of their mind (as
a “technomancer”).</p>
<p>>halfer n. (vulgar) A dwarf.</p>
<p>>hoi interject. (Dutch) Hi, a familiar form of greeting.</p>
<p>>hoop n. (vulgar) A common curse word referring to a person’s backside.</p>
<p>>hose v. Louse up. Screw up.</p>
<p>>ice n. Security software. From “intrusion countermeasures” or IC.</p>
<p>>jack v. To connect or disconnect to the Matrix or other device via a jack. Use jack in to mean establishing the connection, jack out to mean
breaking a connection. Using jack alone refers to changing from one
state to the other.</p>

<p>>jander v. To walk in an arrogant yet casual manner; to strut.</p>
<p>>jing n. Money, usually cash.</p>
<p>>keeb n. (vulgar) An elf.</p>
<p>>kobun n. (Japanese) A member of a Yakuza clan.</p>
<p>>meat n. A physical body. Pertaining to the physical world. Organs
harvested for sale.</p>
<p>>merc n. A mercenary.</p>
<p>>mojo n. (Caribbean) Magic. A spell.</p>
<p>>Mr. Johnson n. Refers to an anonymous employer or corporate agent,
regardless of gender or national origin.</p>
<p>>mundane n. (vulgar) Non-magician. adj. Non-magical.</p>
<p>>nutrisoy n. A cheaply processed food product derived from soybeans</p>
<p>>nuyen n. The world’s standard currency.</p>
<p>>omae n. A close friend. Can be used sarcastically.</p>
<p>>organlegging v. Trading in organs or cyberware harvested from formerly
living people.</p>
<p>>oyabun n. (Japanese) The head of a Yakuza clan.</p>
<p>>pawn n. (derogatory) Street slang for Knight Errant officers</p>
<p>>paydata n. A datafile worth money on the black market.</p>
<p>>pixie n. (vulgar) An elf. An elf poser.</p>
<p>>plex n. A metropolitan complex, short for metroplex.</p>
<p>>poli n. A policlub or a policlub member. adj. Pertaining to a policlub.</p>
<p>>razorgirl n. A female with extensive combat enhancements.</p>
<p>>razorguy n. A male with extensive combat enhancements.</p>
<p>>roke adj. Overly elaborate or unnecessarily detailed. From a shortening
of Baroque.</p>
<p>>samurai n. (Japanese) Mercenary or muscle for hire. Implies an honor
code or a good reputation.</p>
<p>>sarariman n. (Japanese) A corporate employee. From a mispronunciation
of salaryman.</p>
<p>>screamer n. Credstick or other ID that triggers alarms if used.</p>
<p>>scrip n. A currency that is not nuyen, usually referring to currency
issued by a megacorporation.</p>
<p>>simsense n. A sensory broadcast or recording that lets the viewer feel
and experience what the participants feel and experience.</p>
<p>>SIN n. System Identification Number. Identification number assigned to
each person in the society.</p>
<p>>SINless adj. Lacking a SIN. n. A SINless person.</p>
<p>>SINner n. A person with a SIN. An honest person.</p>
<p>>slot n. (vulgar) Mild curse word referring to female genitalia. v. To insert
a chip or credstick into chip or credstick reading device.</p>
<p>>slot and run v. Hurry up. Get to the point. Move it.</p>
<p>>so ka (Japanese) I understand. I get it.</p>
<p>>soykaf n. Ersatz coffee substitute made from soybeans.</p>
<p>>sprawl n. A metroplex (see plex); v. fraternize below one’s social level.</p>
<p>>squat n. Abandoned urban area used for housing. (vulgar) A dwarf.</p>
<p>>squishy n. (vulgar) A dwarf, elf, or human. Usually used by orks and trolls.</p>
<p>>Star, the n. The police. Originally referring to Lone Star specifically.</p>
<p>>static n. Trouble, usually social in nature.</p>
<p>>swag adj. Awesome.</p>
<p>>trideo n. The three-dimensional successor to video. Trid for short</p>.
<p>>trog n. (vulgar) An ork or troll. From troglodyte.</p>
<p>>tusker n. (vulgar) An ork or troll.</p>
<p>>vatjob n. A person with extensive cyberware replacement, reference
is to a portion of the process during which the patient must be
submerged in nutrient fluid.</p>
<p>>wagemage n. A magician (usually mage) employed by a corporation.</p>
<p>>wageslave n. A low-level corporate employee.</p>
<p>>wetwork n. Assassination. Murder.</p>
<p>>wired adj. Equipped with cyberware, especially increased reflexes.</p>
<p>>wiz adj. Wonderful, excellent.</p>
<p>>wizard n. A magician, usually a mage.</p>
<p>>wizworm n. A dragon.</p>
<p>>Yak n. (Japanese) Yakuza. Either a clan member or a clan itself.</p>
<p>>zaibatsu n. (Japanese) A megacorporation.</p>
